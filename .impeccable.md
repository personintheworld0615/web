# Design Context

## Users
BSA Scouts and Troop Leaders. Used both at home (planning) and in the field (mobile).

## Brand Personality
- **Essential**: Only what is needed, nothing more.
- **Precise**: High attention to typography and alignment.
- **Homey**: Warm, cozy, and personal. Feels like a well-worn field notebook or a trusted physical guide.

## Aesthetic Direction
- **Homey Minimalism**: Avoid "Generic SaaS" or "Mechanical Tech" vibes.
- **Palette**: Earthy and Warm (Forest, Oat, Parchment, Clay).
- **Typography**: Editorial and readable (Instrument Serif for warmth, Hanken Grotesk for utility).
- **Theme**: Light/Natural by default (Parchment-like backgrounds).

## Design Principles
1. **NO CORNY FLUFF**: Explicitly forbid thematic "AI slop" or corny roleplay language (e.g., "Instrument", "Protocol", "Calibration", "Diagnostic").
2. **Cozy Precision**: The UI should feel high-end but inviting. Use soft borders, paper-like textures (noise), and warm neutrals.
3. **Task-Centric Minimalism**: Every element must earn its place. If it doesn't help the user generate or find a workbook, remove it.
4. **Cross-Device Reliability**: Ensure a seamless experience between desktop planning and mobile field use. Touch targets must be generous but precise.
5. **Human over Machine**: Use natural language. Avoid "Processing..." if "Preparing your workbook..." feels warmer.
